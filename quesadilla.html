Quesadillas

Quesadillas are a beloved Mexican treat made by folding a tortilla in half and filling it with a generous amount of melted cheese. Traditionally, Oaxaca, Chihuahua, or Manchego cheeses are used, but other cheeses like cheddar or Monterey Jack can also be delicious. For added flavor, you can incorporate a variety of fillings such as grilled meats, saut√©ed vegetables, beans, or salsa. Once filled, the quesadilla is cooked on a griddle or in a skillet until the cheese is gooey and the tortilla is golden brown.

List of ingredients

  • Tortillas (Flour or corn)
  • Cheese
  • Oil or butter

Instructions

  1. Prepare your filling: If you're using meat or vegetables, cook them until they're tender and then set aside.
  2. Heat your cooking surface: Heat a griddle or skillet over medium heat.
  3. Assemble your quesadilla: Place a tortilla on the hot surface. Sprinkle with cheese and your desired filling.
  4. Fold and cook: Fold the tortilla in half, pressing down gently to seal the filling. Cook for 2-3 minutes per side, or until the tortilla is golden brown and the cheese is melted.
  5. Serve: Remove the quesadilla from the heat and cut into wedges. Serve immediately with your favorite toppings, such as salsa, guacamole, sour cream, or cilantro.
